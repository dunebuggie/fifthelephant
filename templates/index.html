    The Fifth Elephant is the premier Indian developer conference on data. It is a place for hackers, and academics at the cutting edge of data science to meet and talk about what they are working on. The goal is to teach, learn and find inspiration among our peers in the art and science of data.

    This edition of The Fifth Elephant will revolve around the two tracks: Discovering Insights and Speed at Scale.

    Discovering Insights is a track about Statistics, Machine Learning and Visualizations - the fundamental conceptual tools that help us excavate, understand, and vividly present actionable information from raw data.

    Speed at Scale is a track about software tools for storage, retrieval and processing of data at large scales under different application requirements. Specifically, about distributed, parallel, or high-performance computing.

    This year's edition of the conference will feature technologies such as Apache Spark, Kafka, Storm, Postgres, Julia, Erlang and R. Participants will also be exposed to theory at the core of today's data driven products -- linear algebra, machine learning techniques, CAP theorem, distributed systems, parallel computing models, hardware acceleration, Graph algorithms, Computer vision, stunning multi-dimensional visualizations, and more.

    2014 Edition

    • 3 days
    • 2 tracks

    The 2012 edition of The Fifth Elephant was the first conference in India which focused on big data and analytics. Followed by that, the 2013 edition also showcased a successful event with big data, storage and analytics.

    The event brought together developers, researchers, scientists, analysts, CTOs, CEOs and academicians who took part in various discussions on big data infrastructure, analytics and application of technology for harnessing untapped potential of data.

    Participants were exposed to some of the popular and emerging technology stacks for storing and processing big data. They learnt about programming languages and libraries available for producing compelling visualisations. Firefox crash reports, Indian Census report, Big data on SSDs, etc were more popular talks. In the analytics track, statisticians and analysts spoke about various tools, techniques, strategies and frameworks for building business intelligence and telling stories with data. Overall, the past editions presented the multiple meanings and facets of big data to participants and helped them in understanding the emerging ecosystem.

    The Fifth Elephant 2012 brought together community and advocacy organisations such as The Centre for Internet and Society, The Internet Archive, Open Knowledge Foundation Network and Wikipedia India chapter to showcase the value of open data and community supported efforts in collecting and publishing public data. The 2013 edition highlighted MongoDB (10gen) and Julia language community. The presence of these organisations facilitated in answering participants’ questions about how to use big data for the benefit of society. The 2014 edition focused on analytics – the infrastructure that powers analytics and how analytics is done.

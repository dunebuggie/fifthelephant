    About The Fifth Elephant

    By 2012, data storage and processing had turned into huge areas of business opportunities and technological challenges. Organizations holding millions of records or even 1 GB of data were grappling with questions about how to store information and retrieve it in the shortest possible time and most efficient manner.

    To respond to this growing trend, HasGeek organized The Fifth Elephant which was to be an annual conference on big data, analytics and application of technology for managing data. The Fifth Elephant 2012 had over 650 participants, across two days, discussing storage, visualization, analytics and application of technology. Participants and speakers were from large and medium-sized companies such as Akamai, Amazon, Enthought, InMobi, Red Hat, Google, IBM, and others. Startups, self-motivated developers, open data communities and researchers also participated in the conference.

    At the The Fifth Elephant 2013, we will help small and large organizations to understand and decide which technologies are required for managing and analyzing data and how these technologies affect business processes and outcomes.

    Big Data

    The Fifth Elephant 2013

    The main themes for the 2013 edition are storage and analytics. There will be separate tracks for technology, decision-support issues and metrics and tools. The agenda is as follows:

    Technology Agenda:

    • Exposure to storage and analytics technologies.
    • Better understanding of why different tools and approaches work for some domains.
    • Interactions with decision-makers and product managers to understand how these technologies fit business processes and goals.

        • Business Agenda:

        • Understand how technology is applied for better customer targeting, building products, improving business processes and decision-making.
        • Interactions with technologists and analysts to learn how data collection and processing techniques work in different sectors.
        • Deeper insights about costs, scale, performance and reliability of technologies for managing and processing data.

        Target Audience:

        The conference is open to developers, architects, database administrators, CTOs, CIOs, product managers and analysts.
